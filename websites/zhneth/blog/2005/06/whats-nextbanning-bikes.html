What's next...banning bikes?!

Today in the Daily Herald it was reported that South Elgin is considering becoming the sixth Chicago-area community to ban the use of motorized scooters on village streets, sidewalks and parks. Where do they expect kids to play? Maybe the appropriate reaction to this situation would be to educate the kids and parents about scooter safety and how to ride responsibly. Taking away another recreational activity for kids is not the answer.

“It’s common sense. They shouldn’t be riding on the sidewalks,” Village Trustee William DiFulvio said Monday night during a discussion on the proposal. “You’ve got little 6-year-old kids riding motorized scooters — it’s getting to be pretty bad.”

Tell me…if they can’t ride on the sidewalks where are they supposed to ride? Their parents’ lawns? The driveway? If this becomes the only place for kids to ride around on their scooters I don’t see the fad (hopefully it’s here to stay) go away. What’s next…banning bikes? Kids ride bikes on sidewalks, in their neighborhood streets and in the parks. I hope this is really thought out before taking away a fun time for a group of kids in South Elgin.
