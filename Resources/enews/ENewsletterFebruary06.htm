Aquatic Facility Operators (AFO) Course
April 18-19 at the Wheaton Park District
Click here to view the PDF Registration Form.
Contact Dina Kartch, IPRA Education & Conference Director for more information.

WHO SHOULD ATTEND?
Aquatic Directors, Recreation Directors, Water Park Directors, Aquatic Supervisors, Aquatic Facility Managers, Pool Managers, Aquatic Design Professionals

COURSE LOCATION:
Wheaton Park District • 1777 S. Blanchard Road • Wheaton, IL 60187 • 630-690-4880

COURSE CONTENT:
The Aquatic Facility Operator course is an 18-hour training program specifically designed to meet the needs of those working in public and semi-public recreational pool or water park facilities. The AFO program offers a unique risk management approach, rarely offered to operations personnel. The course provides information and training for both supervisory and operations personnel. In-depth instruction will be given on filtration, disinfections, water testing and treatment, design consideration, facility management, risk management, facility trouble-shooting and much more. The AFO certification course is the most comprehensive program available for those responsible for operating clean, safe, and efficient aquatic facilities.

CERTIFICATION:
Upon successful completion of the AFO course and a 50-question exam, the National Certification Board of NRPA will officially certify participants. Certification is valid for three years and is renewable.

TEXT:
This course includes a copy of the NRPA’s Aquatic Facility Operator Manual, the most complete and comprehensive publication available on pool operations. Author Kent G. Williams has over 30 years of experience directing beginners and experienced pool managers through the often complex process of aquatic operations.

EARLY REGISTRATION DEADLINE:
March 28, 2006

WIPRA The Voice...It's Like A Radio Show
"If you build it...they will come." A surprising number of you listened and hopefully enjoyed the first podcast (read: radio show) that IPRA launched earlier in January. No, you will not need an iPod or any other mp3 player to listen to the show. IPRA has made listening to the roughly 10-15 minute shows as simple as clicking a button. No downloading files...no installing of third-party programs. Just listen at your computer.
