"It's a Success!"

The first-ever March mailing of this month in...Parks & Recreation was a complete success! Over 70% of you wanted to learn more about the newsletters' offerings and continued to click on various links. Each month we will bring you interesting news, educational seminars and exciting events that you and your agency can get involved with. IPRA has so much to offer and unfortunately not everything can be put into this e-Newsletter. So please visit our website for the most current information and news.

2005 Athletic Gear & Apparel Philanthropy    

The IPRA Athletic Committee is getting ready for the 2005 Athletic Gear & Apparel campaign and will once again need your help to make this successful.

We started this effort and partnership with the United Way in 2003 in order to provide agencies with gently used equipment and apparel. With access to over 2,000 professionals that work at every type of sport, recreation and conservation facility, we have items that are safe and in good working order!

Got a Question? Need an Answer?

One of the benefits of being a member of IPRA is the incredible access to a number of educated and talented park and recreation professionals. If you have a question, big or small, someone in the IPRA network must have an answer.

Discussion boards, like IPRA's Member Forum and newly created Tech KnowHow, are a widely known but under used method for online collaborative learning. The Member Forum and Tech KnowHow can provide its members with an environment for people to communicate, share ideas and strategies about future initiatives and growth of the park and recreation field.

We challenge you to utilize the Member Forum and Tech KnowHow this April. Post questions you may have and respond to questions you may have an answer to. Get the most out of your IPRA membership this month.

??? Did You Know ???

Did you know state law requires government entities to recycle fluorescent lamps?

IPRA has partnered with Fluorecycle, Inc. to offer IPRA member agencies discounted recycling of used fluorescent lamps. Fluorecycle, Inc. would offer park and recreation agencies unit and bulk pricing on their recycling of lamps that contain mercury.

Learn more about Fluorecycle, Inc. here.
